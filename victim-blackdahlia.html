Victim Profile

Biographical details and case information

Elizabeth Short

"The Black Dahlia" | 1924-1947 | Unsolved Murder

Early Life & Background

Birth: July 29, 1924 in Boston, Massachusetts

Family: Fifth of six daughters. Father lost his business during Great Depression and later faked his suicide.

Health Issues: Severe asthma and bronchitis as a child led to hospitalization at age 15.

Early Adulthood: Moved to California in 1942 to live with her father, but they didn't get along. Worked various jobs and moved frequently.

Personality: Described as shy but attention-seeking, often inventing stories about herself. Aspired to be an actress.

Final Days

December 1946: Met Robert Manley, a salesman who became the last known person to see her alive.

January 9, 1947: Checked out of the Figueroa Hotel in downtown LA. Told staff she was going to Chicago.

Whereabouts Unknown: Her activities between January 9-15 remain a mystery. May have stayed with friends or acquaintances.

Final Sighting: Possibly seen at the Biltmore Hotel on January 15, though this remains unconfirmed.

Crime Scene Details

The murder displayed signs of both extreme violence and bizarre ritualistic elements:

Cause of Death

Hemorrhage and shock from blows to the head and facial lacerations

Postmortem Mutilation

Body was completely severed at the waist and drained of blood

Facial Injuries

"Glasgow smile" cuts from mouth to ears, resembling a grotesque smile

Medical Knowledge: The precision of the mutilations suggested the killer had anatomical or surgical training.

Notable Suspects & Theories

George Hodel: LA physician investigated by his own son (an LAPD detective). Had surgical skills and was connected to surrealist circles.

Leslie Dillon: A mortician's assistant with knowledge of embalming. Was in LA at the time but had an alibi.

Mark Hansen: Theater owner who knew Short. His name was cut from her address book found by police.

Unknown Serial Killer: Some link the murder to the similar "Cleveland Torso Murders" or other unsolved crimes.
