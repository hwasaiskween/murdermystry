Chronological breakdown of key events in the investigation

Ted Bundy Investigation

1974-1989 | Multiple States, USA

January 1974

First Known Victim

University of Washington student Lynda Ann Healy disappears from her basement bedroom. This is later confirmed as Bundy's first known murder.

Healy's skull would later be found at Taylor Mountain, one of Bundy's dumping grounds.

November 1974

Utah Abductions Begin

Carol DaRonch narrowly escapes abduction in Salt Lake City after being handcuffed by a man identifying himself as a police officer.

Her detailed description of her attacker and the Volkswagen Beetle he drove would later prove crucial.

August 16, 1975

First Arrest

Bundy is pulled over in Utah and arrested after police find handcuffs, a mask, and other suspicious items in his Volkswagen.

Carol DaRonch positively identifies him as her would-be abductor.

June 7, 1977

First Escape

While awaiting trial in Colorado, Bundy jumps from a second-floor courthouse library window and escapes.

He remains free for six days before being recaptured.

December 30, 1977

Second Escape

Bundy escapes again by crawling through a lighting fixture hole in his cell ceiling.

This time he remains free for nearly two months, during which he commits at least three more murders in Florida.

February 15, 1978

Final Capture

After killing two Florida State University students and attacking several others, Bundy is captured during a routine traffic stop in Pensacola.

Officers find stolen credit cards and other evidence linking him to recent crimes.

January 24, 1989

Execution

After nearly a decade of appeals, Bundy is executed in Florida's electric chair at 7:16 AM.

In his final days, he confesses to numerous unsolved murders across multiple states.
