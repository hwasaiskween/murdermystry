Chronological breakdown of key events in the investigation

Jeffrey Dahmer Investigation

1978-1991 | Ohio & Wisconsin, USA

June 1978

First Murder

Dahmer picks up 18-year-old Steven Hicks while hitchhiking and brings him to his parents' home in Bath, Ohio.

After drinking and sexual activity, Dahmer bludgeons Hicks to death with a barbell when Hicks tries to leave.

September 1987

Murders Resume in Milwaukee

After a nine-year hiatus, Dahmer kills 25-year-old Steven Tuomi in a Milwaukee hotel room.

This marks the beginning of his killing spree in Wisconsin, where he would murder 16 more victims.

May 27, 1991

Tracy Edwards' Escape

Dahmer's intended victim Tracy Edwards escapes and leads police to Dahmer's apartment.

Officers find Polaroids of dismembered bodies, human skulls, and other horrific evidence.

July 22, 1991

Apartment Search

Police discover a human head in Dahmer's refrigerator, seven skulls, and various body parts preserved in chemicals.

Dahmer confesses to 17 murders and provides detailed accounts of his crimes.

January 30, 1992

Trial Begins

Dahmer pleads guilty but insane to 15 counts of murder in Wisconsin.

Psychiatrists testify about his necrophilia, cannibalism, and attempts to create "zombies" by drilling holes in victims' skulls.

February 15, 1992

Sentencing

Dahmer is found sane and sentenced to 15 consecutive life terms (a total of 957 years in prison).

He is later sentenced to a 16th life term in Ohio for Steven Hicks' murder.

November 28, 1994

Death in Prison

Dahmer is beaten to death by fellow inmate Christopher Scarver at Columbia Correctional Institution.

His brain is preserved for study, while the rest of his body is cremated.
