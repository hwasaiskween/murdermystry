Case Investigation Timeline

Chronological breakdown of key events in the investigation

The Zodiac Killer

1968-1974 | Northern California

December 20, 1968

Lake Herman Road Murders

David Faraday (17) and Betty Lou Jensen (16) were shot and killed while parked at a lovers' lane. This was the first confirmed Zodiac attack.

The killer approached their car and fired multiple shots without warning. Police found five spent shell casings at the scene.

July 4, 1969

Blue Rock Springs Attack

Darlene Ferrin (22) was killed and Michael Mageau (19) survived despite being shot multiple times at close range.

The killer called police from a pay phone just minutes after the shooting, saying: "I want to report a murder - no, a double murder."

August 1, 1969

First Zodiac Letters

Three newspapers received identical letters with a cipher and the now-iconic crossed-circle symbol.

The letters contained details only the killer would know, including the type of ammunition used in the attacks.

September 27, 1969

Lake Berryessa Stabbings

The killer wore a black executioner's hood with the Zodiac symbol while attacking Bryan Hartnell and Cecelia Shepard.

He calmly told them he wanted their money and car, then methodically stabbed them multiple times before walking away.

October 11, 1969

Paul Stine Murder

Taxi driver Paul Stine was shot in the head in San Francisco's Presidio Heights neighborhood.

Witnesses saw the killer wiping down the cab interior, showing knowledge of forensic techniques.

October 14, 1969

Bloody Shirt Evidence

The Zodiac sent a piece of Paul Stine's bloody shirt to the San Francisco Chronicle.

Accompanying letters threatened school bus attacks, causing widespread panic and school closures.
