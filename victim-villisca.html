Villisca Axe Murder Victims

June 10, 1912 | Villisca, Iowa

The Victims

Eight people were brutally murdered in the Moore family home:

Josiah Moore (43)

Head of household, local businessman

Sarah Moore (39)

Josiah's wife and mother of four

Herman Moore (11)

Oldest son

Mary Katherine Moore (10)

Daughter

Arthur Moore (7)

Son

Paul Moore (5)

Youngest son

Lena Stillinger (12)

Friend spending the night

Ina Stillinger (8)

Lena's sister, also spending the night

Crime Scene Details

The murders displayed signs of both extreme violence and bizarre ritualistic elements:

Murder Weapon

An axe belonging to Josiah Moore, found at the scene

Method

All victims bludgeoned to death while they slept

Strange Details

Mirrors and windows covered with clothing

Scene Preservation: The killer appeared to have waited in the house for hours after the murders.

Notable Suspects & Theories

Reverend Lyn George Jacklin Kelly: Traveling preacher with a history of violence. Was in Villisca at the time but acquitted.

William Mansfield: Suspected in similar axe murders across the Midwest. Had questionable alibi.

Frank Jones: Local politician and business rival of Josiah Moore. Theory suggests he hired a killer.

Serial Killer: Some believe the murders were part of a series of similar axe murders across multiple states.

Legacy

The Villisca Axe Murders remain one of the most infamous unsolved crimes in American history.

The Moore house still stands and is reportedly haunted, operating as a museum and paranormal investigation site.

The case has inspired books, documentaries, and even an opera.
