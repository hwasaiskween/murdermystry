Psychological and biographical analysis of criminal offenders

Aileen Wuornos

American Serial Killer | Active 1989-1990 | Executed 2002

Early Life & Trauma

Birth: February 29, 1956 in Rochester, Michigan

Abandonment: Her mother abandoned her at birth; father was a convicted child molester who committed suicide in prison.

Childhood Abuse: Raised by abusive grandparents. Began trading sexual favors for cigarettes at age 9, was pregnant by 14 (child given up for adoption).

Teen Years: Lived in woods near her home after being thrown out. Supported herself through prostitution from age 15.

Early Crimes: Arrested for drunk driving, disorderly conduct, and armed robbery in her 20s.

Psychological Profile

Diagnosed Conditions:

  • Borderline Personality Disorder
  • Antisocial Personality Disorder
  • Psychosis (developed during incarceration)
  • Possible Fetal Alcohol Syndrome

Key Traits:

  • Extreme childhood trauma and attachment issues
  • Paranoid ideation (believed men were "raping the planet")
  • Alcohol and substance abuse problems
  • Inconsistent accounts of her crimes (alternated between claiming self-defense and admitting murder)

Murder Methods & Signature Behaviors

Wuornos' killing methods were consistent but her motivations remain debated:

Victim Selection

Targeted middle-aged white male motorists while working as a highway prostitute

Modus Operandi

Lured victims to remote areas, shot them with .22 caliber pistol, stole their vehicles and valuables

Signature

Left bodies at or near the crime scene, made no effort to conceal identities

Financial Motive: Unlike most serial killers, Wuornos appeared motivated primarily by robbery to support herself and her lover.

Capture & Execution

Arrest: Captured in January 1991 after fingerprints linked her to multiple crime scenes.

Trials: Convicted and sentenced to death for six of the seven murders (one case wasn't prosecuted).

Mental Decline: In prison, developed increasingly bizarre behavior, claiming she was being tortured by prison guards with "radio waves."

Execution: After exhausting appeals, was executed by lethal injection on October 9, 2002.

Legacy: One of America's few female serial killers, her case raised questions about mental illness, childhood trauma, and the death penalty.
