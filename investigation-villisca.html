Chronological breakdown of key events in the investigation

Villisca Axe Murders

1912 | Villisca, Iowa, USA

June 10, 1912

Murders Discovered

The bodies of eight victims are discovered in the Moore family home in Villisca, Iowa.

All victims had been bludgeoned to death with an axe while they slept. The killer covered mirrors and windows with clothing.

June 1912

Initial Investigation

Local law enforcement is overwhelmed by the brutality of the crime. The axe is found at the scene.

Several suspicious individuals are questioned but no solid leads emerge.

1914

First Suspect Arrested

Reverend Lyn George Jacklin Kelly is arrested in Oregon for similar murders.

Though he had been in Villisca at the time, he's acquitted due to lack of evidence.

1917

William Mansfield Investigated

William Mansfield, a former suspect in similar axe murders, is investigated but never charged.

His alibi is questionable but authorities lack sufficient evidence.

1920s

Case Goes Cold

Despite multiple investigations and suspects, no one is ever convicted for the murders.

The case remains one of Iowa's most infamous unsolved crimes.
