Case Investigation Timeline

Chronological breakdown of key events in the investigation

Madeleine McCann Disappearance

2007 | Praia da Luz, Portugal

May 3, 2007

Disappearance

Three-year-old Madeleine McCann vanishes from her family's holiday apartment in Praia da Luz, Portugal.

Her parents were dining with friends at a nearby restaurant, checking on the children periodically.

May 4, 2007

Initial Search

Massive search operation begins involving local police, volunteers, and British authorities.

Portuguese police initially treat it as a missing child case, not ruling out abduction.

September 2007

Parents Named Suspects

Portuguese police name Madeleine's parents, Kate and Gerry McCann, as formal suspects (arguidos).

The theory suggests accidental death followed by cover-up, though no evidence supports this.

July 2008

Suspect Status Lifted

Portuguese authorities lift the arguido status of the McCanns due to lack of evidence.

The case goes cold as Portuguese police conclude their investigation.

2011

Scotland Yard Review

UK Metropolitan Police launch Operation Grange to review the case.

New leads emerge, including possible sightings in various countries.

2013

New Suspect Identified

Portuguese police release an e-fit of a man seen carrying a child around the time of disappearance.

The sketch resembles known sex offender in the area at the time.

2020

German Investigation

German authorities name Christian Brückner as a suspect in the case.

Brückner was living in Praia da Luz at the time and has a history of sex crimes.
