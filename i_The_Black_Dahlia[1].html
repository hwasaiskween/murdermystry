The Black Dahlia

January 1947 | Los Angeles, California

Jan 15

Body Discovered

Elizabeth Short's bisected body is found in a vacant lot in Leimert Park, Los Angeles.

The body was cleaned, drained of blood, and posed with hands over head.

Crime Scene Photos Autopsy Report
Jan 21

Media Frenzy Begins

Press dubs victim "The Black Dahlia," sensationalizing the case.
Jan 23

First Letter to Press

Someone claiming to be the killer sends Elizabeth's belongings and a letter to the Examiner.
Jan 25

Suspect Arrested

Robert "Red" Manley detained but released after alibi checks out.
Jan 28

Second Letter

Another letter arrives at the Examiner with the killer taunting police.
Feb 1

Suspect Cleared

Leslie Dillon, a mortician's assistant, is investigated but cleared.
Feb 11

False Confession

Joseph Dumais confesses but is proven to have been in another state.
Mar 1947

Dr. Hodel Investigated

Dr. George Hodel comes under suspicion but isn't charged.
1949

Case Goes Cold

Active investigation winds down without an arrest.
2003

Hodel Theory Resurfaces

Hodel's son, a former LAPD detective, publishes book accusing his father.
