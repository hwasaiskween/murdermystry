Chronological breakdown of key events in the investigation

Black Dahlia Case

1947 | Los Angeles, USA

January 15, 1947

Body Discovered

Elizabeth Short's mutilated body is found in a vacant lot in Los Angeles, severed at the waist and drained of blood.

The body showed signs of careful cleaning, and the killer had arranged her limbs in a posed position.

January 23, 1947

Press Conference

Police hold a press conference announcing they've cleared 25 suspects but have no strong leads.

The media frenzy begins, with newspapers dubbing Short "The Black Dahlia" (though she was never called this in life).

January 25, 1947

Mysterious Call

A man claiming to be the killer calls the Los Angeles Examiner, promising to send Short's belongings.

The next day, an envelope arrives containing Short's birth certificate, photos, and an address book with the name "Mark Hansen" cut out.

February 1947

False Confessions

Over 50 people confess to the murder, overwhelming investigators.

Most are quickly dismissed as attention-seekers or mentally ill individuals.

April 1949

Case Goes Cold

After two years of intense investigation with no viable suspects, the LAPD officially classifies the case as cold.

The case file grows to over 750 pages with hundreds of suspects interviewed.
