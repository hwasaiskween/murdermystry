Chronological breakdown of key events in the investigation

Aileen Wuornos Investigation

1989-1990 | Florida, USA

November 30, 1989

First Victim Found

The body of Richard Mallory, a 51-year-old electronics store owner, is found in a wooded area of Volusia County, Florida.

He had been shot four times with a .22 caliber weapon. This would later be identified as Wuornos' first victim.

June 1, 1990

Multiple Victims Discovered

Police discover the bodies of David Spears and Charles Carskaddon, both shot multiple times with the same .22 caliber weapon.

The similar MO leads investigators to suspect a serial killer targeting middle-aged men along Florida highways.

July 4, 1990

Peter Siems Disappearance

65-year-old missionary Peter Siems vanishes after leaving for a trip to Arkansas. His bloodstained car is found abandoned on July 17.

Wuornos would later claim to have killed Siems, though his body was never found.

August 4, 1990

Final Victim

Walter Antonio is found shot to death in Dixie County, becoming the sixth and final victim linked to Wuornos.

Ballistics match the same .22 caliber weapon used in the other murders.

January 9, 1991

Arrest

Wuornos is arrested at The Last Resort biker bar in Volusia County after a bartender recognizes her from police sketches.

Her girlfriend, Tyria Moore, cooperates with police and provides crucial evidence.

January 16, 1991

Confession

After initially denying involvement, Wuornos confesses to all seven murders, claiming each was in self-defense against rape.

She provides detailed accounts of each killing, though forensic evidence contradicts her self-defense claims.

October 9, 2002

Execution

After 12 years on death row and multiple appeals, Wuornos is executed by lethal injection at Florida State Prison.

Her final words: "I'd just like to say I'm sailing with the rock, and I'll be back like Independence Day with Jesus. June 6, like the movie. I'll be back."
