Jeffrey Dahmer

The Milwaukee Cannibal | Active 1978-1991 | Died 1994

Early Life & Family Background

Birth Name: Jeffrey Lionel Dahmer

Born: May 21, 1960 in Milwaukee, Wisconsin

Childhood Trauma: Dahmer exhibited disturbing behavior from an early age, including animal cruelty and fascination with dead animals. His parents' tumultuous marriage and eventual divorce deeply affected him.

Education: Graduated from Revere High School in Ohio in 1978. Briefly attended Ohio State University before dropping out.

Early Signs: Dahmer was socially isolated and developed an alcohol dependency in his teens. His first murder occurred shortly after his high school graduation.

Psychological Profile

Diagnosed Conditions:

  • Borderline Personality Disorder
  • Schizotypal Personality Disorder
  • Psychotic Disorder (during incarceration)
  • Alcohol Use Disorder

Key Traits:

  • Extreme necrophilia and cannibalistic fantasies
  • Fear of abandonment and extreme loneliness
  • Desire to create "zombie" companions
  • Compulsive need to preserve body parts as trophies

Manipulative Behavior: Dahmer was able to convince police to return a 14-year-old victim to his apartment by claiming the boy was his lover, leading to the victim's murder.

Murder Methods & Signature Behaviors

Dahmer's crimes were characterized by extreme sexual deviance and postmortem activities:

Victim Selection

Primarily targeted young men, often racial minorities, from gay bars and bus stops

Modus Operandi

Lured victims with money or alcohol, then drugged and strangled them

Signature

Engaged in necrophilia, cannibalism, and preservation of body parts

Escalation: Dahmer progressed from standard murders to attempting to create "living zombies" by drilling holes in victims' skulls and injecting acid.

Capture & Death

Arrest: Captured on July 22, 1991 after victim Tracy Edwards escaped and alerted police, leading to the discovery of horrific evidence in Dahmer's apartment.

Confessions: Initially confessed to 17 murders, providing detailed accounts of his crimes and motivations.

Prison Sentence: Received 15 consecutive life sentences (957 years total) plus an additional life sentence in Ohio.

Death: Beaten to death by fellow inmate Christopher Scarver on November 28, 1994 while serving his sentence.
